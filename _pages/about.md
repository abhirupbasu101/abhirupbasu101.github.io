Hello! I am a math PhD student at the University of Arizona. I work at the [New Frontiers of Sound](https://newfos.arizona.edu/) which is an NSF funded Science and Technology Center focusing on the emerging field of topological acoustics. My advisor is [Dr. Pierre Deymier](https://mse.engineering.arizona.edu/faculty-staff/faculty/pierre-deymier). More about my background is in my [CV](/cv/).

My current research focus is on exploring quantum-like phenomena in classical systems and implementing quantum analogue computations on an acoustic platform that uses "phibits" (acoustic analogue of qubits). I have also worked on acoustic topological insulators, multiband topology in acoustic systems and ways to engineer topology in composite systems. You can find more information about my research [here](/publications/).

My broader interests include nonlinear dynamics in general, probability theory, and information theory. Currently, I am also looking into photonic information processing.
